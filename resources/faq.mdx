<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How long does it take to immigrate to Canada?">
    Express Entry can take as little as 6 months from profile to PR. Other pathways (PNP, family sponsorship) can take 12–24 months or more.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need to speak English or French?">
    Yes — most immigration and study programs require an official language test (IELTS, CELPIP, or TEF).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How much money do I need?">
    It depends. For a study permit, expect CAD 20,000\+/year. For Express Entry, IRCC publishes minimum settlement funds based on family size.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can my family come with me?">
    Yes. Spouses can often get an open work permit and children can attend public school for free.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the difference between a visa and a permit?">
    A visa lets you enter Canada. A permit (study/work) lets you stay and do specific activities.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is healthcare free in Canada?">
    Public healthcare is funded by taxes, but coverage starts only after you become a resident — sometimes after a waiting period. Private insurance is recommended for newcomers.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
